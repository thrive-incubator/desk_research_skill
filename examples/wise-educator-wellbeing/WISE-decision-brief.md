# Stage 1 Brief: WISE Educator Wellbeing

_A spin-out of the consultation, credentialing, and capacity-building layer of Georgetown/MedStar WISE's tiered (MTSS-style) educator-wellbeing framework, sold to K-12 districts and states. Recommendation: CONDITIONAL GO · Likely path: spin-out with guardrails._

## Recommendation

Advance to Stage 2. The mission fit is excellent, the why-now is sharp and dated, and the inbound demand is real — but the go is conditional on de-risking three things in customer discovery: that interest converts to funded, recurring revenue; that the evidence base behind the "research authority" moat is real and citable; and that the model can be productized beyond a labor-bound consulting practice. The single condition that decides it: **can at least one inbound buyer — start with Philadelphia / Education Plus Health — be turned into a quoted, funded contract for the educator-wellbeing layer specifically?**

## Why (the three things that matter)

- **Why-now is genuine.** The ESSER cliff (September 2024), the ~$1B cancellation of federal school-mental-health grants (April 2025, only $208M restored by December 2025), and a 1,071-to-1 student-to-school-psychologist ratio make a "build local capacity, don't add headcount you can't fund" framework well-timed.
- **Market read — fragmented, not empty.** "No competitor at scale" is false. Student-MH incumbents (Effective School Solutions, Cartwheel, Care Solace, Hazel) own the student budget; RISE/Kaiser (free), CharacterStrong, and Consult4Ed already sell educator wellbeing. Real defensible white space exists — clinical-research-grade *organizational* wellbeing as a tiered system — but the wedge must beat free content plus the existing EAP plus inertia, not a vacuum.
- **The biggest risk.** Willingness-to-pay is grant-shaped and unproven as recurring revenue — every named buyer is currently *seeking funding*, not signing. If inbound interest doesn't convert, there is no business.

## Market and buyer at a glance

- **Size:** TAM ~$4–8B/yr (US K-12 PD + SEL + school-MH); SAM ~$300–600M; SOM ~$3–9M/yr, bottom-up (≈100 schools × $25–75K, or districts × $150–400K, plus a clinician credential at $1.5–5K each). Sizes like a mid-range $3–15M, non-VC business — if productized. (Medium confidence.)
- **Who pays:** credible first dollar is a foundation-backed district pilot (Philadelphia / EPH); the durable, scalable dollar is a state MTSS-academy contract. Districts (Title II-A PD plus whole-child budgets) sit in between.
- **Price anchor:** the category is quote-based and opaque — a Stage-2 gap. Comparables: EAP $12–40/employee/yr; district MH contracts $15K–$240K; external PD ~$2K/teacher; clinician certs ~$375/credit.

## Mission and studio fit

Squarely on-mission (the workforce that supports children's mental health, with student spillover as the thesis) and a fit for the sustainable, non-VC posture. Two issues need an internal call before Stage 3. First, the Attune/Attunify boundary: structurally a near-twin (consultation + credentialing + Georgetown research); the clean split is K-12/MTSS vs. 0–5/IECMH plus shared services, but it must be decided before either goes customer-facing. Second, spin-out vs. spin-in plus brand/IP: the WISE brand and likely the IP sit under MedStar, not Thrive, and the offering depends on ongoing Georgetown/MedStar clinical delivery — which, like Thrive Academy, pulls toward a possible spin-in. ("A Wise spinout" already sits on the Tracker's on-hold list.)

## Biggest open question and what we'd test next

Does inbound interest convert to funded, recurring revenue — and is the evidence base real? Cheapest, highest-leverage Stage-2 sequence: (1) get Megan's published or in-progress research on the educator → culture → student chain (this gates the whole moat); (2) settle the Attune boundary plus the spin-out/spin-in and WISE brand-IP question at the gate committee, with Neal and Shai and OTC; (3) pull three to five real price quotes and verify the Philadelphia economics; (4) interview 8–10 of the "100+ inbound" to separate politeness from funded intent. A "no" on funded conversion *or* on the evidence base flips this to NO-GO. See the in-depth report for the full analysis and sources.
